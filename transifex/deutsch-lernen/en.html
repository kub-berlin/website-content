Learning German

Enrollment

Our courses run for one semester:
from mid-October to the end of March (winter semester)
or from mid-April to the end of September (summer semester).

You will learn best if you join for the whole semester. However, it is also possible to join during the semester.

You can enroll in our courses at consultation hours. You need an appointment for the that. You can enroll for summer semester from March and for winter semester from September.

Consultation hours

During the consultation we will check with you which level you need and if our courses are suitable for you. If there are free places, you can enroll. Or you can get advice about other German courses in Berlin.

To make an appointment for consultation, come to KuB or send an email to dk-anmeldungen@kub-berlin.org.

How can you join?

Everybody can learn German with us. Classes are free of charge. You do not have to pay anything. It does not matter how old you are. It does not matter which residency status you have.

There are several courses

  • Alphabetization: You will learn how to read and write. This is your first German course.
  • A 1.1: In this course for beginners you already know how to read and write. You do not speak any German. Here you will learn how to speak and understand German. This is your first German class.
  • A 1.2: You already speak a little German. This is your second German course.
  • A 2.1: You already speak a little German. This is your third German course.
  • A 2.2: You do speak German quite well. This is your fourth German course.
  • B 1: Your 5th, 6th, or 7th German course.
  • B 2: You do not have any problems in your everyday life. With B2 you are allowed to study at a University.
  • Conversation Course: There is also a course for conversation. Here you focus on your German conversation.
  • Mother-Child-Course: You are a mother to a small child. In our mother-child-course you learn German. We do take care of your child while you are in the German course.

Information

  • One course is 5 months long.
  • Most courses are held 2 times a week.
  • Each session goes for 2 hours.
  • You want to prove that you speak German for the Jobcenter or a job application? Sorry, but we cannot do this! We do not hand out official certificates about your German knowledge.
  • But we can write a document that you are participating in our courses.
  • We don't have many spots available. People who cannot easily attend other German courses are more likely to get a spot.

Day trip once a month

We do have day trips once a month.

We walk through the centre of Berlin, go to a museum or to a park.
